If you've ever built a record-triggered Flow by hand, you know the dance: pick the object, configure the trigger, drag a Decision element onto the canvas, name three resources you'll forget about in a week, and click Save four times.

Claude Code skips all of that.

## What we're building

A Flow that fires when a Lead's `Region__c` changes, and routes the Lead to a different owner per region.

## The prompt

```
Build a record-triggered Flow on Lead. When Region__c changes,
reassign Owner based on region:
  - "NA" → user@example.com
  - "EMEA" → user2@example.com
  - "APAC" → user3@example.com
Anything else stays unchanged.
Deploy to dev-org.
```

That's it. Claude reads your `sfdx-project.json`, generates the Flow XML, and deploys it.

## Why this matters

You used to spend 20 minutes clicking. Now you spend 30 seconds typing. Multiply that by every Flow your team builds this quarter and you start to see why admins who learn this workflow stop volunteering for "quick" Flow tickets in standup.

> The point isn't speed for its own sake. It's that a 20-minute task no longer pulls you out of deep work.
